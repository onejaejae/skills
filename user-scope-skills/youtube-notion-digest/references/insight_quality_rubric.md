# Insight Quality Rubric (A-Z)

Use this rubric before finalizing the digest or publishing to Notion.

If 5 or more letters fail, rewrite.

## A-Z checks

- `A Audience`: Is the target audience explicit?
- `B Baseline`: Does it compare before/after or current/target state?
- `C Context`: Is context sufficient for understanding decisions?
- `D Decision`: Does each major section improve a real decision?
- `E Evidence`: Are major claims traceable to timestamps?
- `F Falsifiability`: Can at least one core claim be tested or disproven?
- `G Granularity`: Are points specific enough to execute?
- `H Horizon`: Is time horizon explicit (now/sprint/quarter)?
- `I Insight`: Are insights non-obvious, not paraphrases?
- `J Judgment`: Is there clear adopt/watch/reject judgment?
- `K Knowledge gain`: Would a reader learn something new?
- `L Limits`: Are boundary conditions and failure cases stated?
- `M Metrics`: Do proposed actions include measurable success criteria?
- `N Novelty`: Is at least one idea surprising but defensible?
- `O Operational`: Are there concrete next actions?
- `P Prioritization`: Are top insights clearly ranked?
- `Q Questions`: Are open questions decision-relevant?
- `R Risk`: Are key risks mapped to mitigation or stop rules?
- `S Signal`: Is signal density high (low filler)?
- `T Tradeoff`: Are explicit tradeoffs presented?
- `U Uncertainty`: Is uncertainty level communicated honestly?
- `V Verifiability`: Can another person verify the claims from transcript?
- `W What-next`: Does it specify next 30-day experiments?
- `X eXternal`: Is external validation used only when needed and labeled?
- `Y Your-context`: Is content tailored to the user's context?
- `Z Zero-fluff`: Could at least 20% of text be removed without losing value?

## Hard fail conditions

Fail the draft immediately if any of these happen:

- no decision matrix
- no boundary condition in insights
- no counterpoint section
- timestamps are present but not tied to conclusions
- output claims `notion-visualizer` usage without actually invoking it
